What is Personality Disorder ?


Personality disorder is a type of mental disorder characterized by unhealthy patterns of behavior, thoughts, and emotions. These patterns are deeply ingrained and tend to be inflexible, leading to problems in relationships, work, and other areas of life.People with personality disorders may have difficulty forming and maintaining relationships, may struggle with their self-image, and may have trouble managing their emotions. They may also have difficulty understanding and empathizing with others, and may struggle with impulsive behavior.

There are many different types of personality disorders, each with its own unique set of symptoms and diagnostic criteria. In this article, we'll discuss some of the types of this health condition and also discuss how to treat it.

Some of the most common types include :


  • Borderline personality disorder: People with borderline personality disorder may struggle with unstable relationships, intense emotions, and impulsive behavior.
  • Narcissistic personality disorder: People with narcissistic personality disorder may have an inflated sense of self-importance, may lack empathy for others, and may have a constant need for admiration.
  • Antisocial personality disorder: People with antisocial personality disorder may have a disregard for the rights of others, may engage in criminal behavior, and may lack empathy for others.
  • Obsessive-compulsive personality disorder: People with obsessive-compulsive personality disorder may struggle with perfectionism, rigidity, and an excessive need for control.
  • Schizotypal personality disorder: characterized by odd beliefs, magical thinking, and eccentric behavior.


Diagnosis of a personality disorder usually involves a comprehensive evaluation by a mental health professional, which may include a psychological evaluation, a review of the person's medical history and symptoms, and a discussion with family members or loved ones about the person's behavior and symptoms.
Treatment of personality disorders may include therapy, medication, and support groups. Cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) can help people with personality disorders learn coping strategies and develop new ways of thinking and behaving. Medications such as antidepressants or mood stabilizers may also be used to manage symptoms.

It is important for people with personality disorders to have ongoing support and treatment, as symptoms can be chronic and can impact many areas of their life. With the right treatment and support, people with personality disorders can learn to manage their symptoms and live a fulfilling life.
